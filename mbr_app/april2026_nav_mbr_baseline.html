  • Traffic drove most of the decline — Nav engagement held steady. Total Nav clicks fell -3.8% MoM, but sitewide traffic was down 2.6%. That means most of the drop was simply fewer people visiting — not fewer people engaging with Nav. Out of every 1,000 visitors, roughly 23 clicked into Nav in both March and April. The engagement rate barely moved.
  • Customers are shifting from winter to spring — and Nav should follow. The three department entries that showed strong April growth all point in the same direction: Party Supplies (+11.5%), Sports & Outdoors (+4.7%), and Patio & Garden (+3.1%) within Home Garden Tools. Meanwhile, winter-heavy categories like Clothing (-12.6%) and indoor Home L1s are naturally pulling back. May strategy: lean into summer L1s (swimwear, sandals, outdoor furniture), graduation gifting, Mother's Day, and Memorial Day.
  • Sub Nav click decline is driven by seasonal pill swaps, not broad erosion. Removing the Easter pill — which drove massive click volume in March — created the single largest drop in Sub Nav. We also swapped Dinner Made Easy → Meals Made Easy, broadening from one meal occasion to all meals positioning — but the pill did not resonate with customers. Those two changes alone explain the bulk of the shortfall. The remaining legacy pills — Rollbacks & More, Walmart+, New Arrivals, My Items, Credit Card, Pharmacy, and Only at Walmart — were roughly flat. We're currently running a Mother's Day seasonal pill and expect it to pull Sub Nav clicks back up through May.
⚠️ April Caveats & Business Context
  • Easter — Only a few days fell in April. March had the full seasonal window, creating tough comps across Dept Nav and Sub Nav.
  • Mother’s Day — Flipped into Dept Nav on April 5. N/A MoM. Already generating 343K App / 291K rWeb Sub Nav clicks.
  • Collectibles — New Dept L0 test launched April 15. Early engagement is low (9.1K App clicks, 0.09% share, Exit Rate 50.9%). Category is too granular for an L0 entry point.
  • Walmart Business — In test as a Dept L0 since April 15. Only 50% of traffic seeing it. Not reflected in full-month metrics.

📉 The Flip Side: Winter / Indoor Categories
The biggest click losses are concentrated in winter L1s across Clothing (-207K App clicks) and indoor Home categories
👗 Clothing, Shoes and Accessories (-12.6% App, -11.2% rWeb)
The single largest absolute click loss in Nav: -207K App / -53.5K rWeb. The decline is driven by winter collection L1s winding down — Easter dresses & formalwear (March peak gone), outerwear & jackets, boots & cold-weather footwear, and kids' clothing (post-Easter gap). Summer L1s (swimwear, sandals, active) haven't ramped enough to offset yet.
🏠 Indoor Home L1s (Home Garden Tools post-winter normalization)
Four indoor L1s account for the Home Garden Tools decline: Home Decor -9.5K clicks (-12.6%), Kitchen Dining -7.7K (-9.0%), Furniture -5.2K (-6.1%), Bedding -3.2K (-7.5%). Post-winter refresh and indoor entertaining demand fading as customers shift attention outdoors.

Spring Home drove 17.3K App clicks and 43.4K rWeb clicks with a healthier exit rate (37.2% App / 31.3% rWeb), though GMV/Click remains modest ($0.22 App / $0.58 rWeb).

📉 Sub Nav ATC -37%: Driven by Seasonality and Traffic Drop
The headline ATC drop is driven by seasonality and traffic drop:
· 🐰 Easter wind-down accounted for ~637K lost ATC (full month Mar → few days Apr)
· 🍽️ Dinner Made Easy → Meals Made Easy swap cost ~225K ATC as the replacement ramps — ATC dipped from 250K to 322K and GMV dropped ~$100K
· ⚡ Get It Fast declined ~176K ATC from reduced engagement

These 3 transitions = ~92% of the total ATC shortfall. The remaining legacy pills were approximately flat, and the sitewide traffic pullback (-2.6% sessions) further compressed pill volumes across the board.
